The Bell Jar is written from the perspective of Esther Greenwood, a college student navigating her role as a woman in 1950s society. Her brilliance grants her opportunities, such as funding from a sponsor, Mrs. Guinea. However, as the novel progresses, she spirals deeper into her depression. About the Author
Sylvia Plath was born in 1932 in Massachusetts. Her books include the poetry collections The Colossus, Crossing the Water, Winter Trees, Ariel, and The Collected Poems, which won the Pulitzer Prize. She was married to the poet Ted Hughes, and died in London in 1963.
